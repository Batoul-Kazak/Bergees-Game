Based on the image, this game is called **Checkers** (known as **Draughts** in the UK and many other countries).

Specifically, looking at the 8x8 board, the 12 pieces per side, and the website printed at the bottom ("usacheckers.com"), this is **American Checkers** (also called English Draughts). The numbers on the board are used for recording moves in tournaments.

Here are the rules in brief:

**1. The Objective**
The goal is to win by either capturing all of your opponent's pieces or blocking them so they have no legal moves left.

**2. Movement**
*   Pieces (men) move only on the dark squares.
*   A regular piece moves diagonally forward one square at a time to an empty space.

**3. Capturing (Jumping)**
*   If an opponent's piece is diagonally adjacent to yours and the square immediately behind it is empty, you must jump over it.
*   The jumped piece is removed from the board.
*   **Mandatory Jumps:** In standard rules, if a capture is available, you *must* take it. You cannot make a regular move if a jump is possible.

**4. Kings**
*   When a piece reaches the furthest row on the opposite side of the board, it becomes a "King."
*   Kings can move and capture diagonally both **forward and backward**.
*   (In physical sets, this is usually done by stacking a second piece on top of the first one).

**5. Multi-Jumps**
*   If after jumping a piece, you land in a position where you can jump another piece, you must continue jumping in the same turn until no more jumps are possible.
